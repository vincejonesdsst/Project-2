Story of Kobe Bryant

Career of the G.O.A.T

Kobe Bean Bryant is a former professional basketball who played his entire 20-year career with the Los Angeles Lakers of the National Basketball Association (NBA). He entered the NBA directly from high school and won five NBA championships in his 20-year career with the Los Angeles Lakers of the National Basketball Association . He entered the NBA directly from high school and won five NBA championships with the Lakers. Bryant is an 18-time All-Star, 15-time member of the All-NBA Team, and 12-time member of the All-Defensive team. He led the NBA in scoring during two seasons, and ranks third on the league's all-time regular season scoring and fourth on the all-time postseason scoring list. He is only one of the few players to ever come straight to the NBA out of High School. Kobe attended Lower Marion High School in Pennslyvania.

Best Game and Final Season

Kobe Bryant's 20 season career came to a devastating end in the 2015-2016 when he announced that he would be retiring at the end of the season. His season prior to that last season were filled with injuries and his age really did start to show in his health and in his game. In the end of the 2014-2015 season Kobe Bryant tore his right Achillies in the final minutes of a very important Warriors-Lakers game. His final season he averaged 17.6 points per game and ended his career with a 60 point game against the Utah Jazz. Kobe was celebrated and honored before and after the game as he holds the record for most seasons played with one team. Kobe spent a good amount of his season injured and sidelined with shoulder injuries and with a knee injury but Kobe did make a point to play his final away games in all the different cities to allow the fans to pay their farewells.

Life After Basketball

In an interview with Kobe Bryant, he discusses his plans after he retires from the NBA. He discusses how he intends to spend time with his 2 daughters and will continue to stay in shape even if he is not currently playing. Kobe Bryant became a commentator for professional basketball after he retired so that he could stay around the game and still be involved. He also stated in one of post game interviews after his amazing 60 point that he would have strong interest in joining the front office for an organization and even possibly coaching at the NBA level.
